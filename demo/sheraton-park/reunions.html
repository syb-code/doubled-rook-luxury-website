What a Weekend Here Looks Like

Plain answers for volunteer planners.

You shouldn’t need a procurement department to book a reunion. Here’s the honest shape of it: a block of rooms, a welcome dinner, a hospitality suite, and a farewell brunch — priced clearly, contracted simply.

Family reunions

Blocks of 10–40 rooms, two-queen depth for the cousins, welcome-dinner and farewell-brunch packages — and the parks a walk away. Summer dates book 3–6 months out.

Military reunions

Hospitality suites for the storytelling, banquet nights for the ceremony, per-diem-friendly and government rates available — and a destination the whole family actually wants to visit.

Faith retreats & parish events

A longtime host hotel for Anaheim’s largest annual faith gatherings, with retreat-friendly meeting space, banquet capacity, and weekday flexibility for formation days and jubilees.

Fraternal & service organizations

District meetings and regional gatherings for Elks, Rotary, Lions, and Greek-letter organizations — banquet-led, value-priced, drivable for SoCal chapters.

Timing Tip

Flexible dates, better rates.

If your group can travel November through January, you’re booking into the hotel’s most flexible window — that’s when value-minded reunions and retreats get the property’s best combination of rate, space, and attention.

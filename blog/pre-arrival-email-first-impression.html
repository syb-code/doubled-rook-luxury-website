The first impression is the email three days before arrival

June 16, 2026 1 min read By Michael Gomez Guest Experience · Hotel Marketing · Hospitality

The first impression of your hotel isn't the lobby. It's the email three days before arrival and most hotels waste it on a bland confirmation.

By the time a guest reaches your front desk, you've already had three days to shape their stay. The pre-arrival window is the most underused moment in hospitality.

What a strong pre-arrival sequence does:

  1. Sets expectations. Check-in time, parking, directions, what to bring; all questions answered before they have to ask. Fewer questions at the desk, smoother arrival.
  2. Offers digital check-in. Let guests share arrival time and details in advance. Your team preps the room and the welcome; the guest skips the queue.
  3. Opens the upsell and concierge door. Early check-in, a room upgrade, dinner reservations; all offered when the guest is excited, not exhausted.
  4. Sets the tone. A warm, well-written message signals the kind of stay they're about to have.

This isn't technology for its own sake. It's removing friction and starting the relationship before the guest walks in.

Audit yours this week: forward yourself your own confirmation email. Does it build anticipation, or just confirm a transaction?

Persona 01 · The check-writer

Owner & Asset Manager

Optimizes for return on capital, downside protection, and comp-set performance. Sees marketing as a line on the P&L and wants to know it earns its keep. Skeptical of agency promises and allergic to lock-in.

Existing VendorsCost & ROI
The objection
I'm already paying my management company and brand for marketing. Why pay you on top?
What's underneath

Fear of duplicate spend. Assumes the flag and the manager already cover this ground.

The counter

Brand and management marketing is built for the flag's averages, not your property's specifics — it rarely touches your vanity site, local SEO, OTA content and ads, AEO/GEO, or lifecycle email at the property level. We operate the levers your property actually controls, and charge no commission on media, so the spend you're already running works harder without a percentage skim. The kickoff audit shows exactly where the brand program stops and the gaps begin.

Say this

The brand markets the flag. We market your four walls — and we don't take a cut of your media.

Cost & ROIMeasurement
The objection
Show me the ROI. How do I know this returns more than it costs?
What's underneath

The owner needs the math to pencil and is wary of agency vagueness dressed up as confidence.

The counter

We don't lead with a promised number — we lead with a baseline. The engagement opens with a tracking audit, comp-set review, and opportunity map, so within the first weeks you can see your current direct mix, the leaks, and the realistic upside before the engine scales. A flat fee with zero media commission means your cost is fixed and predictable, so ROI is measured against a known number, not a moving percentage.

Say this

I'll show you the baseline and the leaks first. You decide if the upside pencils — before the spend scales.

Cost & ROITiming
The objection
Marketing is the first thing I cut when the market softens.
What's underneath

Treats marketing as discretionary cost rather than demand insurance.

The counter

A soft market is exactly when direct mix and tight attribution protect margin — every point you shift off OTA commission drops to the bottom line when you need it most. Cutting marketing usually means leaning harder on OTAs at 15–25% commission right when you can least afford it. And the tiers let you scale down to Foundations rather than going dark.

Say this

In a soft market, the cut isn't marketing — it's the OTA commission you pay when you go dark.

Trust & Proof
The objection
Every agency promises direct-booking lift. What makes yours real?
What's underneath

Heard the pitch before. Pattern-matches all agencies as interchangeable.

The counter

Most agencies promise the outcome and bill on a percentage of the spend that produces it — their incentive grows with your budget. We charge a flat fee with no media commission, so we're paid the same whether your spend is $5k or $50k. The only way this works for us is if the efficiency improves. And the plan is built from the operator's seat — the founder ran this work property-side through turnaround portfolios — not from a generic agency template. These aren't projections: recent engagements include a 44% revenue lift with a near-doubled conversion rate, and a turnaround that reversed an eight-month decline — both published above.

Say this

Other agencies get paid more when you spend more. We don't. Our only lever is making it work better.
